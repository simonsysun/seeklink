# Pad Thai

Classic Thai street-food stir-fried rice noodles. The sauce is the
thing — everything else is just ingredient handling.

## Ingredients (serves 2)

For the sauce:
- 3 tbsp tamarind paste
- 2 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 2 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp soy sauce

For the stir-fry:
- 200g flat rice noodles, soaked in room-temperature water for 30 min
- 2 eggs
- 150g firm tofu, cubed and pan-fried golden
- 100g shrimp (optional)
- 2 green onions, chopped
- 1 cup bean sprouts
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tbsp peanut oil
- Crushed peanuts, lime wedges, chili flakes for serving

## Method

1. **Make sauce**: whisk tamarind, palm sugar, fish sauce, soy sauce in
   a bowl until sugar dissolves. Taste — should be equal parts sweet,
   salty, sour. Adjust.
2. **Prep**: have all ingredients at hand, the stir-fry is fast.
3. **Heat wok**: high heat, add oil. Swirl.
4. **Eggs**: crack eggs in, scramble quickly, push to one side.
5. **Garlic + protein**: garlic, then tofu/shrimp, 30 seconds.
6. **Noodles + sauce**: drained noodles in, pour sauce over, toss hard
   to coat every strand. If sticking, add a splash of water.
7. **Finish**: bean sprouts, green onions, toss 30 seconds.
8. **Plate**: peanuts, lime wedge, chili flakes on top. Serve immediately.

## Common mistakes

- **Over-soaking noodles**: they should bend but not be soft. They
  finish cooking in the wok.
- **Wok not hot enough**: noodles stick and go gummy. You need the
  hiss and small flare when sauce hits.
- **Too much sauce**: noodles should be *coated*, not swimming.
- **Tamarind substitute**: lime juice works but the flavor is not the
  same. Try to find real tamarind paste.
