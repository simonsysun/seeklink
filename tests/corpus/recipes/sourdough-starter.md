# Sourdough starter from scratch

Takes 7-10 days to build a viable starter. No yeast, no commercial
cultures. Just flour, water, and wild yeast from the air.

## Day 1

- 50g whole-wheat flour + 50g water (room temp, filtered — chlorine
  kills wild yeast). Mix in a clean jar, cover loosely, leave on
  counter.

## Day 2

- Probably no visible activity. Smell will shift slightly. Feed:
  discard half, add 50g flour + 50g water.

## Day 3

- Might see some bubbles. Might not. Feed again: discard half, add
  50g AP flour (switching from whole wheat) + 50g water.

## Day 4-7

- Twice-daily feeds: every 12 hours, discard half, feed 50g+50g.
- Day 4-5 may show lots of activity then die down (the first "false
  bloom" is often the wrong bacteria). Don't panic.
- Day 6-7 you want a reliable pattern: 2-3× volume within 6 hours of
  feeding, smells yeasty/tangy/pleasant.

## Testing it's alive

The **float test**: drop a spoonful in water. If it floats, it's
active. If it sinks, feed and wait.

## Using it

Once active:
- Feed 4-6 hours before baking (at peak — highest rise).
- Typical bread uses 20% of flour weight in mature starter.
- Store in fridge if not baking daily; feed once a week.

## What can go wrong

- **Pink/orange streaks**: contamination. Throw it out, start over.
- **Harsh ammonia smell**: starving. Feed more frequently.
- **Liquid on top ("hooch")**: hungry but not dangerous. Pour off and
  feed.
- **No rise after 10 days**: your flour, water, or environment isn't
  working. Try rye flour or a different water source.
